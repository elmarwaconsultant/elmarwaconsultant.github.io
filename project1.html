Edko Crossing Project:
A Landmark in Infrastructure

The Edko Crossing Project is a transformative infrastructure initiative, designed to transport untreated agricultural wastewater from Edko, Bersiqa, and Abu Qir to the main drain. With an impressive capacity of 3.5 million cubic meters per day, the project addresses critical challenges in agricultural wastewater management, ensuring sustainable and efficient water utilization in the region.

OUR ROLE IN THE PROJECT

As the engineering consultant, El-Marwa Construction Designs played a pivotal role in the successful completion of the Edko Crossing Project.

Our contributions spanned multiple aspects of the project, ensuring seamless execution and adherence to the highest standards of quality and safety.

Scope of Work

  • Hydraulic and Structural Studies: Conducted comprehensive hydraulic studies and developed structural designs for the entire path to overcome obstacles and ensure smooth execution.
  • Execution Drawings and Technical Reports: Prepared all necessary execution drawings and detailed technical reports, ensuring clarity and precision in every aspect of the project.
  • Design of Required Crossings: Engineered specific crossings to facilitate smooth execution during pipeline installation along the project path.
  • Water Collection Tanks: Designed high-capacity tanks to collect and channel water under the roadway, ensuring efficient water management throughout the project.
  • Retaining Walls: Developed retaining wall designs to support the International Coastal Road during construction, ensuring structural stability and safety.
  • Foundation Design for Gates and Mechanical Components: Engineered robust foundations to support gates and other mechanical components within the shafts used for tunneling operations.
  • Advanced TBM Setup Design: Designed and implemented a setup for the Tunnel Boring Machine (TBM) to push pipes with a 4-meter diameter along a 279-meter line at a depth of 7 meters below ground.
  • Integration of BIM Technology: Leveraged Building Information Modeling (BIM) technology to integrate structural, mechanical, and electrical designs.
  • Comprehensive Risk Assessments: Conducted detailed risk assessments to address challenges during shaft sinking and groundwater drainage, ensuring the safety of operations and equipment.
  • Cost Estimation and Quantity Surveying: Performed accurate cost calculations and quantity surveys to ensure efficient resource allocation within the project's budget.
  • Monitoring and Documentation: Closely monitored subcontractors‚Äô work and maintained meticulous documentation of completed tasks.

Supervision and Ownership

  • Supervising Authority: The project was overseen by the Engineering Authority of the Armed Forces ‚Äì Water Directorate, ensuring adherence to safety and quality standards.
  • Owner: The Ministry of Water Resources and Irrigation, responsible for managing and utilizing water resources across Egypt.
  • Main Contractor: Elsewedy Infra, a subsidiary of Elsewedy Electric, served as the main contractor for the project. Their extensive expertise in infrastructure projects was crucial to the successful execution of the Edko Crossing Project.
  • Consultant: El-Marwa Construction Designs provided design consultancy, technical support, and on-site guidance, working in collaboration with Elsewedy Infra to ensure project success.
